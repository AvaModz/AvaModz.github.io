Government Structure

Executive Branch

Led by the President. Oversees executive departments, enforces laws, and conducts foreign policy.

Click to view details

The Executive Branch directs the day-to-day administration of the federal government. It is responsible for enforcing legislation, managing cabinet departments and agencies, issuing executive actions, handling national policy priorities, and representing the nation in domestic and foreign affairs.

  • Led by the President and supported by executive departments and White House staff
  • Implements and administers laws passed by the legislature
  • Coordinates national policy, security, diplomacy, and agency oversight

Legislative Branch

Comprised of the Senate and House of Representatives. Responsible for drafting, debating, and passing legislation.

Click to view details

The Legislative Branch serves as the lawmaking body of rUSA. Members introduce bills, debate policy, review amendments, and vote on proposals that shape the direction of government. This branch also provides oversight and serves as a central arena for political negotiation.

  • Includes both the Senate and House of Representatives
  • Creates, debates, amends, and passes legislation
  • Acts as a major check on executive authority

Judicial Branch

Headed by the Supreme Court. Interprets constitutional matters and settles legal disputes through trials and judicial review.

Click to view details

The Judicial Branch interprets the Constitution, reviews disputes, and ensures that laws and government actions remain consistent with the governing legal framework of rUSA. Its role is vital in settling disputes, clarifying constitutional questions, and protecting the integrity of the system.

  • Led by the Supreme Court and supported by judicial officers
  • Conducts constitutional review and legal interpretation
  • Resolves disputes through hearings, rulings, and formal decisions
